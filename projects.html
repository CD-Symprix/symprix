Commercial Due Diligence

Commercial Due Diligence for Major High-Speed Rail Investment – France — Certares, 2025

Context

Symprix supported IPEX, acting on behalf of global investment firm Certares, in evaluating a strategic investment in a European open-access high-speed rail operator competing on major French HSR corridors, including the high-density Paris–Lyon axis, one of the most important and commercially significant high-speed routes in Europe.

Given the capital intensity and competitive dynamics of high-speed rail, Symprix was engaged to independently review the operator’s revenue forecasts and commercial assumptions, assessing the credibility of demand projections, pricing segmentation, load factor targets, and the robustness of the revenue-driven profitability case.

Contribution

  • Provided an independent commercial review of the operator’s revenue strategy and underlying business assumptions.
  • Assessed whether projected passenger growth and pricing positioning were credible within a competitive high-speed market.
  • Identified key revenue risks and areas of sensitivity relevant to long-term financial sustainability.

Impact

  • Delivered an independent expert assessment of the robustness and credibility of revenue assumptions.
  • Highlighted areas of potential optimism bias in demand capture and premium segment contribution.
  • Strengthened investor understanding of revenue sufficiency and commercial risk prior to investment decision-making.
Rolling Stock Evaluation

Modernising Passenger Valuation in UK Rail Demand Forecasting — Rail Delivery Group, 2026

Context

Symprix supported CPCS, acting as prime consultant to the Rail Delivery Group (RDG), in delivering a nationally significant study to evaluate how passengers perceive and value rolling stock attributes. The project was commissioned under the Passenger Demand Forecasting Council (PDFC) to inform potential updates to the Passenger Demand Forecasting Handbook (PDFH), the industry’s core demand modelling framework.

The study addressed long-standing questions about the robustness of Stated Preference (SP) evidence and the need to strengthen forecasting guidance using real-world Revealed Preference (RP) data. It examined emerging passenger priorities — including comfort, connectivity, accessibility, and perceptions of new versus refurbished rolling stock — and assessed whether existing PDFH multipliers remain behaviourally credible in today’s operating environment.

Contribution

  • Led the strategic design of an evidence-based framework to reassess how rolling stock attributes influence passenger demand.
  • Advised RDG on strengthening the reliability of existing valuation guidance within PDFH.
  • Integrated behavioural evidence and industry data to support more credible forecasting assumptions.

Impact

  • Strengthened the strategic foundation of rolling stock valuation within national demand forecasting guidance.
  • Introduced the structured use of Service Quality Regime (SQR) operational data into econometric demand analysis, expanding the industry evidence base.
  • Enabled more credible business cases and investment decisions through behaviourally grounded valuation evidence.
